# Model Definition: Food Hub (Ireland)
**Full Name:** Regional Food Hub / Community Enterprise Centre  
**Countries:** Ireland 

## Legal Status
* **Entity Type:** Most commonly structured as a **CLG (Company Limited by Guarantee)** or a **Social Enterprise**. It is a non-profit-distributing entity.
* **Fiscal Nature:** Professional commercial activity. It manages high-volume transactions, VAT for multiple producers, and often receives government grants (e.g., via LEADER or Enterprise Ireland).
* **Institutional Goal:** To act as a "Virtual and Physical Anchor" for small-scale producers, providing them with the scale of a supermarket but the ethics of direct sales.

## Juridical References
* **FSAI Standards (Food Safety Authority of Ireland):** The Hub acts as the primary responsible body for HACCP compliance in shared spaces.
* **EU Social Enterprise Framework:** Alignment with European standards for the Social and Solidarity Economy (SSE).
* **Industrial & Provident Societies Act:** Used by hubs structured as traditional cooperatives.

## Business Logic
The Irish Food Hub model is a hybrid of "Software as a Service" and "Physical Infrastructure":

1.  **Shared Production (Incubation):** The Hub rents out "Time Slots" in certified commercial kitchens. This lowers the entry barrier for new producers.
2.  **Aggregation Logic:** Instead of 50 farmers driving to 50 delivery points, they all drive to one Hub. The Hub manages the "Last Mile" logistics.
3.  **The "Market Host" System:** A coordinator (Host) manages the digital storefront, customer service, and the physical collection day, allowing farmers to focus solely on production.
4.  **Commission-Based Sustainability:** Typically, the producer keeps 80-85% of the price. The remaining 15-20% stays at the Hub to pay for staff, energy, and the digital platform.
5.  **B2B & B2C Flow:** The same inventory is used to serve individual families (weekly bags) and local businesses (wholesale supply to pubs and restaurants).

## Workflow & Identity
* **The "Sort" (Logistics Heart):** A precise operation where bulk deliveries from producers are split into individual customer crates based on digital packing lists.
* **Click-and-Collect:** The primary identity is a weekly community event. The Hub is a "Social Anchor" where people meet during the pickup window.
* **Traceability:** Every item is digitally tagged back to the specific artisan producer, ensuring 100% transparency.
* **Shared Resource Management:** Beyond food, the workflow includes managing the booking calendar for kitchens, cold rooms, and shared delivery vans.
