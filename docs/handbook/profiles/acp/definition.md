# Model Definition: ACP
**Full Name:** Agriculture Contractuelle de Proximité  
**Countries:** Switzerland (mainly Romandie), France (border regions)

## Legal Status
* **Entity Type:** Usually a **Cooperative** or a **Non-profit Association**. It is strictly regulated by a "Contract of Proximity" between a group of consumers and a farm.
* **Fiscal Nature:** Non-commercial partnership. The price is based on the "fair cost of production" rather than market speculation.
* **Institutional Goal:** Formalizing the commitment of citizens to support local peasants, ensuring a fair wage and sustainable environmental practices.

## Juridical References
### National & Regional Framework
* **Switzerland: FRACP (Fédération Romande d'Agriculture Contractuelle de Proximité):** The primary regulatory body that grants the ACP label and defines the mandatory charter.
* **Charter of the FRACP:** Defines the 5 pillars: Direct relationship, Commitment, Transparency, Peasant Farming, and Solidarity.
* **Swiss Federal Law on Agriculture:** General framework for direct payment and agricultural support.

## Business Logic
The ACP model is a highly formalized version of the CSA, focusing on the legal "Contract":

1.  **The Contract (L'engagement):** A formal, legally binding document signed annually. The consumer commits to purchasing the entire production share for the year, and the farmer commits to transparency and ecological methods.
2.  **Price Transparency (Le prix de revient):** The budget is calculated based on the actual costs of the farm. Members don't pay a "market price" but a share of the production costs (labor, seeds, social contributions for the farmers).
3.  **Mandatory Participation (Heures de participation):** Almost all ACPs require members to contribute labor (usually 2-4 half-days per year) as a structural part of the contract to lower production costs.
4.  **No Selection (Le panier imposé):** Unlike a shop, the consumer has no choice in the content of the basket. They receive what is ripe and available, strictly following the seasonality of the Swiss terroir.
5.  **Direct Payment (Fair Trade):** 100% of the price goes to the farm. The association/cooperative is funded by a separate membership fee (*cotisation*) to ensure zero margin on the food itself.

## Workflow & Identity
* **Contract Management:** The software must prioritize the management of signed PDF/Digital contracts and their annual renewals.
* **Labor Tracking:** A crucial technical module to track the "Participation hours" for each member/household.
* **Distribution Points (Points de chute):** Managing the logistics of local collection points, often managed by the members themselves on a rotational basis.
* **Transparency Dashboard:** Tools to show members exactly how their "subscription" covers specific farm costs (e.g., "Your share paid for 10 hours of farmer's wages this month").
