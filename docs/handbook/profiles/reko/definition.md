# Model Definition: REKO
**Full Name:** Rejäl Konsumtion (Fair Consumption)  
**Countries:** Sweden, Finland, Norway (Origin), expanding to Italy and the UK.

## Legal Status
* **Entity Type:** Typically an **Informal Network** or a "Circle" (*REKO-ring*) managed via social media groups. It does not usually have a formal legal personality as a group, but the individual producers must be registered businesses.
* **Fiscal Nature:** Direct-to-consumer sales. Each transaction is a direct contract between the producer and the consumer. The REKO-ring itself acts only as a non-commercial meeting facilitator.
* **Institutional Goal:** To eliminate middlemen and create a local, transparent marketplace with zero infrastructure costs for producers.

## Juridical References
### National & International Framework
* **EU Reg. 178/2002:** Standard European food safety and traceability applied to direct sales.
* **Primary Production Exemptions:** Utilization of local regulations that allow small producers to sell directly to consumers without the burdens of retail labeling or permanent shop infrastructure.
* **National Consumer Protection Laws:** Standard distance-selling regulations (as orders are placed online).

## Business Logic
The REKO model is a "Flash-Market" system designed for maximum logistical efficiency and zero overhead:

1.  **Digital Pre-Ordering:** All sales happen before the physical meeting. Historically, this occurs within a Facebook Group or a dedicated platform (like *Local Food Nodes*). Producers post their available products, and consumers comment/order.
2.  **No On-Site Sales:** There is no "browsing" or cash-handling for unplanned purchases at the delivery point. This avoids the legal requirements of a traditional market (peddler licenses, food stall permits).
3.  **The "Delivery Window":** Producers and consumers meet at a pre-arranged public location (parking lot, square) for a very short, specific time (e.g., 30 to 60 minutes).
4.  **Drive-Through Logistics:** The workflow is optimized for speed. Producers park their cars/vans, and consumers walk or drive from one vehicle to another to collect their pre-paid or pre-ordered bags.
5.  **Zero Commission:** The REKO-ring administrators are volunteers. No fees are taken from the producers or the consumers for the use of the platform/network.
6.  **Producer Autonomy:** Each producer is responsible for their own invoicing, payment collection (often via mobile apps like Swish in Sweden or Satispay in Italy), and compliance.

## Workflow & Identity
* **Event-Centric:** The software must manage "Delivery Events" with strict start/end times and location mapping.
* **Producer-Managed Inventory:** Unlike GAS, where a referent manages products, in REKO, the producer has full control over their digital "stall" and stock levels.
* **Rapid Check-in:** The system focuses on providing consumers with a single "Collection List" (QR code or digital receipt) to show to multiple producers in one session.
* **Social Discovery:** The platform acts as a discovery engine for local food, allowing users to find "Rings" near them and join specific delivery dates.
