# Model Definition: GAS
**Full Name:** Gruppo di Acquisto Solidale  
**Countries:** Italy

## Legal Status
* **Entity Type:** Non-profit Unincorporated Association (*Associazione di fatto*) or Registered Association (*Associazione riconosciuta*).
* **Fiscal Nature:** Non-commercial entity. Activities are classified as "internal distribution for institutional purposes" among members, not as retail trade.
* **Institutional Goal:** Collective sourcing for ethical, solidarity, and environmental sustainability.

## Juridical References
### National Framework
* **Legge Finanziaria 2008 (L. 244/2007), Art. 1, commi 266-268:** The primary legal recognition of GAS in Italy, defining them as non-profit entities for solidarity purchasing.
* **D.P.R. 633/1972, Art. 4:** Exclusion from VAT (IVA) for non-commercial activities conducted by associations for their members.
* **D.Lgs. 117/2017 (Codice del Terzo Settore):** Governance and fiscal reporting standards for GAS operating as *Associazioni di Promozione Sociale (APS)*.
* **EU Reg. 178/2002:** General Food Law application for internal traceability within non-professional food distribution chains.

### Regional Frameworks (Selection)
* **Lombardia:** L.R. 2/2010 (Promotion of Solidarity Economy).
* **Toscana:** L.R. 58/2012 (Support for Solidarity Economy and GAS).
* **Emilia-Romagna:** L.R. 19/2014 (Development of Solidarity Economy).

## Business Logic
The GAS model operates on a non-speculative circular workflow designed to disintermediate the supply chain:

1.  **Producer Selection:** Members collectively vet suppliers based on ethical, ecological, and local criteria (Short Supply Chain).
2.  **Referent Assignment:** Every supplier is managed by a "Referent" (a volunteer member) who acts as the bridge for communications, order opening, and delivery coordination.
3.  **Collective Ordering Window:** Orders are opened for a specific timeframe. Members place orders, often aiming to reach "minimum crates" or bulk thresholds to optimize transport.
4.  **Bulk Breakdown (Ripartizione):** Since goods often arrive in bulk (e.g., 10kg crates), the logic requires dividing these units among individual member shares.
5.  **Variable Weight Adjustment:** For products like cheese or meat, the final price is adjusted in the ledger *after* the physical weighing during distribution.
6.  **Prepaid Social Fund (Cassa Comune):** Financial flows are managed through a common fund where members deposit advances to allow the GAS to pay producers promptly, often before or upon delivery.
7.  **Social Tax (Reciprocity):** A small percentage is added to the producer's price to fund the association's internal costs (rent for the distribution space, insurance, software hosting).

## Workflow & Identity
* **Referent Logic:** Decentralized peer-to-peer management; each referent is responsible for a specific producer's lifecycle.
* **Prepaid Wallet:** Systematic use of internal credit to facilitate micro-payments and ensure zero-debt toward producers.
* **Social Tax:** A contribution (usually 1-5%) legalized as an "associative contribution" to maintain group infrastructure.
