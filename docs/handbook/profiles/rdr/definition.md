# Model Definition: Rede de Reciprocidade
**Full Name:** Redes de Reciprocidade / AMAP Portugal  
**Countries:** Portugal (inspired by the global CSA/AMAP movement)

## Legal Status
* **Entity Type:** Informal collectives or "Associações sem fins lucrativos". 
* **Fiscal Nature:** Based on "Mutual Aid" (Ajuda Mútua). The transaction is often framed as a contribution to the farm's survival rather than a purchase of a product.
* **Institutional Goal:** To create a direct, transparent, and fair link between "Prosumers" (Prosumidores) and "Producers" (Produtores), sharing both the risks and the bounties of the land.

## Business Logic: Reciprocity & Trust
The Portuguese model focuses on the "Moral Contract" beyond the technical one:

1.  **Commitment (Compromisso):** Members commit to a full agricultural season (usually 6 to 12 months). Payments are made upfront or in installments to provide the farmer with a stable income.
2.  **Shared Risk:** If a crop fails due to weather, the members share the loss. If there is a surplus, everyone enjoys the abundance.
3.  **Transparency:** Total transparency regarding production costs. The price is often discussed and agreed upon collectively to ensure it covers a dignified life for the farmer.
4.  **Prosumption:** Members are not just "consumers"; they are encouraged to participate in farm life, visits, and decision-making processes.
5.  **No Intermediaries:** Total exclusion of middle-men. The logistics are managed by the group (Nùcleo) through voluntary work.

## Workflow & Identity
* **The "Nùcleo" (The Node):** Each group of reciprocity is a "Nùcleo". It is a sovereign cell that manages its own logistics and relationship with one or more producers.
* **Cestas (Baskets):** Delivery of weekly or bi-weekly baskets of seasonal products.
* **Participation:** Mandatory or encouraged participation in "Montarias" or "Chantiers" (collective work days on the farm).
