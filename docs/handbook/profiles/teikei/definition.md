# Model Definition: Teikei
**Full Name:** Teikei (提携 - "Partnership" or "Cooperation")  
**Countries:** Japan

## Legal Status
* **Entity Type:** Mostly organized as **Consumer Collectives** or informal partnerships. Often linked to the *Japanese Consumers' Co-operative Union (JCCU)* or independent organic farming associations.
* **Fiscal Nature:** Direct-to-consumer non-commercial distribution based on a "producer-consumer pact."
* **Institutional Goal:** To provide "Food with a Face." It was born in the 1970s as a reaction to industrial pollution (Minamata disease) and the loss of food safety.

## Juridical References
### National Framework
* **Japan Organic Agriculture Association (JOAA):** Founded in 1971, it established the "Ten Principles of Teikei," which serve as the moral and operational constitution for these groups.
* **Consumer Co-operative Law:** Provides the legal framework for more formalized consumer collectives in Japan.
* **Agricultural Land Act:** General regulations regarding small-scale farming and direct distribution.

## Business Logic
The Teikei model is defined by a deeply philosophical and philosophical approach to the relationship between the land and the city:

1.  **The Ten Principles:** Unlike Western models that focus on logistics, Teikei is governed by 10 principles (JOAA), including "Mutual Assistance," "Self-distribution," and "Democracy."
2.  **Pre-financing & Commitment:** Consumers provide the capital upfront to support the farmer's yearly planning. There is a total commitment to consume the entire production of the farm.
3.  **Non-Selection (The "Osekkai" concept):** Members do not choose what they receive. They accept the "blessings of nature" in their entirety, including products that might be considered "ugly" or non-standard by commercial markets.
4.  **Producer-Consumer Logistics:** In many Teikei groups, the consumers themselves organize the transport and the "Han" (small local cells of 5-10 families) to distribute the products, reducing the labor burden on the farmer.
5.  **Direct Dialogue:** Frequent face-to-face meetings and visits to the farm are mandatory to build "Face-to-Face" trust, which replaces third-party organic certification.
6.  **Fair Price through Negotiation:** Prices are not set by the market but through a transparent negotiation between the producer and the collective, ensuring the farmer's livelihood.

## Workflow & Identity
* **"Han" System Management:** The software must manage a hierarchical structure: the Farm -> the Teikei Group -> the "Han" (local distribution units).
* **Trust-Based Ledger:** High focus on transparency and communication logs rather than just transactional data.
* **Seasonal Planning:** Tools for farmers to share their "Yearly Production Plan" with the collective for feedback and approval.
* **Collective Distribution:** Features to coordinate member-led logistics and pick-up shifts within the "Han."
