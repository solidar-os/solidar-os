# Model Definition: RWS (Poland)
**Full Name:** Rolnictwo Wspierane przez Społeczność  
**Countries:** Poland (and growing in Central Europe)

## Legal Status
* **Entity Type:** Mostly operates as an **Informal Agreement** between a farmer and a group of consumers, or via **Foundations/Associations** (*Fundacje/Stowarzyszenia*).
* **Fiscal Nature:** Often falls under **RHD** (*Rolniczy Handel Detaliczny* - Agricultural Retail Trade) or **Direct Sale** (*Sprzedaż Bezpośrednia*). These are simplified fiscal regimes in Poland for small-scale farmers.
* **Institutional Goal:** To build trust between urban consumers and local farmers, ensuring the survival of small traditional Polish farms (*Gospodarstwa rodzinne*).

## Juridical References
### National Framework
* **Poland: Ustawa o gospodarstwie rodzinnym:** General support for family-scale farming.
* **Poland: Rozporządzenie dot. RHD (Agricultural Retail Trade):** Provides the legal limit and tax-free thresholds for farmers selling directly to consumers.
* **Social Economy Law (2022):** New legislation in Poland supporting social enterprises and cooperative movements.

## Business Logic
The Polish RWS model often acts as a bridge between the radical SoLaWi bidding and a more commercial subscription model:

1.  **Direct-to-Farmer Contracts:** Similar to AMAP, members sign seasonal commitments. However, these are often managed by a "Group Coordinator" (local leader) who facilitates the logistics.
2.  **Pickup Points (Punkty Odbioru):** Distribution is highly urban-centric. Farmers deliver to specific "host" apartments, garages, or small eco-shops in cities like Warsaw, Krakow, or Wroclaw.
3.  **Variable Pricing for Accessibility:** Some groups implement a "Social Fund" to subsidize shares for lower-income members, though the "Bieterrunde" is less common than in Germany.
4.  **Volunteer Engagement:** Members are expected to help with the "packing" or "sorting" of vegetables at the pickup point, reducing the farmer's labor time.
5.  **Focus on Bio/Eko Certification:** There is a high technical demand for verifying certificates (Organic/EKO) within the platform, as trust in "natural" labeling is a key driver in the Polish market.

## Workflow & Identity
* **Trust-Based Ledger:** The software must prioritize transparency regarding the farmer's certifications and the origin of each product.
* **Urban Logistics:** Managing multiple small-scale pickup points within a city with strict delivery windows.
* **Coordinator Dashboard:** A specific interface for the "Local Leader" to manage their specific group's accounting and member communications.
