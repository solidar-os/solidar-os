# Model Definition: GdC
**Full Name:** Grupos de Consumo Responsable / Cooperativas de Consumo  
**Countries:** Spain

## Legal Status
* **Entity Type:** Consumer Cooperative (*Cooperativa de Consumidores y Usuarios*) or Non-profit Association (*Asociación sin ánimo de lucro*).
* **Fiscal Nature:** Social Economy Entity (*Entidad de la Economía Social*). Transactions are generally considered internal distribution among cooperative members.
* **Institutional Goal:** Transformation of the food system through the *Economía Social y Solidaria (ESS)*, promoting food sovereignty and agroecology.

## Juridical References
### National Framework
* **Ley 27/1999 de Cooperativas:** The general framework for national-level cooperatives.
* **Ley 5/2011 de Economía Social:** Recognition of social economy entities as pillars of general interest.
* **EU Reg. 178/2002:** Standard European food traceability requirements applied to direct-to-consumer delivery hubs.

### Regional Frameworks
* **Catalonia:** Llei 12/2015 de Cooperatives de Catalunya (Highly advanced framework for *Consum Cooperatiu*).
* **Andalusia:** Ley 2/1999 de Cooperativas de Andalucía.
* **Madrid:** Ley 2/2023 de Cooperativas de la Comunidad de Madrid.

## Business Logic
The GdC model is characterized by a high degree of "Autogestión" (Self-management) and community involvement:

1.  **Assembly-Based Governance:** Strategic decisions, supplier selection, and pricing policies are determined in periodic Assemblies (*Asambleas*).
2.  **Shared Responsibility (Turnos):** Unlike the individual "Referent" model, GdC members often take turns performing mandatory tasks, such as cleaning the hub, coordinating deliveries, or managing the "Local".
3.  **The "Local" (Distribution Hub):** A physical space (often a shared warehouse or social center) where products are delivered, stored briefly, and picked up. The software must manage access and logistics for this specific point.
4.  **Cesta Logic (Fixed Basket):** Many groups operate via "Cestas Cerradas" (weekly subscriptions of seasonal produce) to ensure the farmer has a stable income regardless of specific item demand.
5.  **Multi-Tier Ordering:** A hybrid system allowing a core subscription (Cesta) plus "Pedidos Abiertos" (extra items like oil, wine, or dry goods) managed in different order cycles.
6.  **Transparency Ledger:** Total visibility of margins, logistic costs, and cooperative surplus to all members, often integrating "Social Currency" or internal ethical credit.

## Workflow & Identity
* **Autogestión:** High transparency and collective accountability; roles are often rotational rather than fixed.
* **Cooperative Ledger:** Management of member contributions and shared surpluses (*Retornos cooperativos*).
* **Local de Reparto:** The workflow is centered around the physical hub's operational window (Distribution hours).
