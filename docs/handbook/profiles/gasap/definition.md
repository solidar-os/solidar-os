# Model Definition: GASAP
**Full Name:** Groupes d'Achat Solidaires de l'Agriculture Paysanne  
**Countries:** Belgium (Brussels & Wallonia)

## Legal Status
* **Entity Type:** Typically an **ASBL** (*Association sans but lucratif*) at the network level, with local groups operating as informal citizen collectives linked by a common charter.
* **Fiscal Nature:** Non-commercial. It operates through "Direct Contracts" between members and producers, where the group serves only as a logistical and social facilitator.
* **Institutional Goal:** Supporting peasant agriculture through long-term commitments and fostering "Food Citizenship" (*Citoyenneté alimentaire*) in urban and peri-urban contexts.

## Juridical References
### National & Regional Framework
* **Belgium: Law of 27 June 1921 (ASBL):** The legal framework for the non-profit associations that coordinate the network.
* **Charte des GASAP:** The founding document (inspired by the AMAP charter but autonomous) that defines the ethical obligations of producers and members.
* **Réseau GASAP:** The coordinating body in Brussels and Wallonia that provides the logistical and political framework for individual groups.

## Business Logic
The GASAP model emphasizes the **Group-Producer relationship** as a long-term partnership:

1.  **Direct Annual Contract:** Members commit to a full season (usually one year) with one or more producers. Payments are made in advance or via standing orders to guarantee the farmer's income.
2.  **Collective Management (Autogestion):** Each GASAP is self-managed. Members share administrative tasks: one handles accounting, another the communication with the farmer, and another the delivery logistics.
3.  **The "Local" (Point de Ravitaillement):** Unlike some French AMAPs that meet at the farm, GASAPs are heavily urban. Distribution happens in shared "Points de chute" (garages, community centers, cafes).
4.  **No Intermediary / No Commission:** 100% of the price goes to the producer. The network and local groups are funded via separate, transparent membership fees.
5.  **Fixed Basket + Extras:** The core is the "Seasonal Basket," but the GASAP logic often integrates "Extras" (honey, cheese, flour) managed via separate recurring contracts within the same group.
6.  **Network Solidarity:** Groups are not isolated; they are part of a federation (*Le Réseau*) that manages the legal defense of the model and the search for new producers.

## Workflow & Identity
* **Group-to-Producer Mapping:** The software must link specific citizen groups to specific farmers, managing the "one-to-one" or "one-to-many" contractual relationship.
* **Contractual Ledger:** Management of seasonal commitments and payment installments (standing orders tracking).
* **Role Rotation:** A technical module to manage the internal tasks of the group (e.g., "Who is on duty for the next distribution?").
* **Logistics Hubs:** Managing urban delivery windows and access codes for shared distribution points.
