# Model Definition: Food Coop
**Full Name:** Participatory Food Cooperative / Supermercato Cooperativo  
**Countries:** Global

## Legal Status
* **Entity Type:** Legally incorporated as a **Consumer Cooperative** (e.g., *Società Cooperativa* in Italy, *SCIC* in France, *IPS* in Ireland).
* **Fiscal Nature:** Professional retail activity. Unlike informal groups, it has a physical VAT-registered premise, employees (minimal), and complex accounting.
* **Institutional Goal:** To eliminate the profit margin of traditional retail by turning "customers" into "owners" and "workers," ensuring access to high-quality food at near-wholesale prices.

## Juridical References
* **ICA Principles:** Adherence to the 7 International Cooperative Principles (Democratic Control, Economic Participation, etc.).
* **Social and Solidarity Economy (SSE) Law:** Often benefits from specific national frameworks for social enterprises.
* **Retail Food Regulations:** Must comply with all standard commercial health, safety (HACCP), and labor regulations.

## Business Logic: The "Triple Role"
The Food Coop model relies on members fulfilling three simultaneous roles:

1.  **The Owner (Member):** Every shopper must buy a "share" (capital) of the coop. This provides the startup and working capital, removing the need for bank loans or external investors.
2.  **The Worker (Member-Labor):** Inspired by the *Park Slope Food Coop* (NYC), members typically commit to **~3 hours of work every 4 weeks**. This unpaid labor covers 75-90% of operational tasks (stocking shelves, cleaning, cashiering), drastically reducing overhead.
3.  **The Customer (Exclusive Access):** In many models (e.g., *La Louve* in Paris, *Bees Coop* in Brussels), **only members** are allowed to shop. This creates a closed-loop economy and a strong sense of community belonging.

## Workflow & Identity
* **Physical Storefront:** Unlike a GAS, it is a permanent shop open several days a week.
* **Shift Management:** The critical logistical challenge is the scheduling of hundreds or thousands of member-volunteers.
* **Transparent Pricing:** Usually applies a flat markup (e.g., fixed 10-20%) on all products, just enough to cover fixed costs (rent, utilities, and a few paid coordinators).
* **Product Selection:** Decided democratically through committees, prioritizing local, organic, and ethical sources.
