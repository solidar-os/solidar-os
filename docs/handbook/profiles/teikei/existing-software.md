# Existing Software: Teikei (Japan)

This document maps the digital landscape of the Japanese Teikei movement, where the philosophy of "Co-partnership" (Teikei) often prioritizes human trust over automated logistics.

## 1. Specialist Software & Networks
The Japanese ecosystem is unique due to the presence of massive consumer cooperatives and local farmer-led networks.

| Name | Status | Technical Notes | Link |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Seikatsu Club System** | **Active** | Proprietary/Custom. One of the largest consumer co-ops in the world. Manages complex collective orders and internal quality standards. | [seikatsuclub.coop](https://seikatsuclub.coop) |
| **Daichi wo Mamoru Kai** | Active | Custom E-commerce. A professionalized network that pioneered organic distribution in Japan. | [daichi-m.co.jp](https://www.daichi-m.co.jp) |
| **Teikei Hub (Internal)** | Limited | Many individual Teikei groups use small, custom-built databases or PHP scripts to manage seasonal member lists. | - |

## 2. Multi-Model Platforms
Global platforms are less dominant in Japan due to linguistic barriers and the highly specific nature of Japanese delivery logistics (Takkyubin).

| Name | Status | Technical Notes | Link |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Open Food Network (JP)** | **Emerging** | Managed by local food sovereignty advocates. It is the bridge for groups seeking international standards and open-source sovereignty. | [openfoodnetwork.jp](https://openfoodnetwork.jp) |
| **Tabechoku** | Active | (Commercial SaaS) A direct-to-consumer platform that mimics some Teikei logic but with a modern, high-commission e-commerce approach. | [tabechoku.com](https://www.tabechoku.com) |

## 3. Communication & Social Tools
* **LINE Groups:** The primary communication channel in Japan for group coordination, replacing WhatsApp/Signal.
* **Paper-based Ledger (Kakeibo style):** Many traditional Teikei groups still use physical notebooks or simple Excel sheets to maintain the "human touch" in accounting.
* **Furusato Nozei Portals:** While governmental, these platforms have accustomed Japanese citizens to "gift-based" regional food support, influencing the UX of modern food hubs.
